# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Engineers define quality standards, design and execute test plans, and validate that software meets acceptance criteria before and after release. They act as the quality gate across the entire delivery lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain test plans, test cases, and acceptance criteria in collaboration with Developers and Product Managers
- Perform manual and automated testing (unit, integration, end-to-end, regression)
- Triage and track defects, coordinating with Developers on root cause and resolution
- Produce quality evidence and release-readiness reports
- Champion a Definition of Done that includes testability and observability

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Increase test coverage and reduce regression risk
- Enable the team to release with confidence and predictability

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement to review acceptance criteria
- Bug triage meetings and defect reports
- Release sign-off checklist and QA status updates

### Collaboration
- **Developers**: Pair on writing testable code and automating test suites; align on acceptance criteria early in each sprint.
- **Product Managers**: Clarify edge cases and expected behavior to ensure test coverage reflects real user scenarios.
- **Project Managers**: Report quality metrics and release readiness; flag blockers that affect the delivery timeline.

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, translate them into intuitive interfaces, and validate designs through usability testing. They ensure the product is both functional and delightful to use.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research (interviews, surveys, usability tests) to surface insights and validate assumptions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define and maintain a shared design system or UI guidelines
- Collaborate in requirement sessions to embed user-centered thinking early
- Gather and analyze usability feedback post-release

### Goals
- Maximize usability, accessibility, and user satisfaction
- Reduce rework by aligning design and engineering before implementation begins
- Establish a consistent, accessible visual and interaction language

### Typical Communication
- Discovery workshops and design critiques with Product Managers and Developers
- Design handoff documentation (annotations, component specs)
- Usability test readouts and design iteration summaries

### Collaboration
- **Developers**: Provide design specifications and participate in implementation reviews to ensure fidelity; answer design questions during sprints.
- **Product Managers**: Translate product goals and user research into design direction; validate priorities through data and prototypes.
- **Project Managers**: Communicate design milestones and flag scope changes that affect timelines; ensure design work is tracked on the project board.

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain clear, accurate documentation for users, developers, and operators. They bridge the gap between subject-matter expertise and audience understanding.

### Responsibilities
- Author and update user-facing docs (guides, FAQs, release notes) and developer-facing docs (API references, runbooks)
- Establish and enforce documentation standards, templates, and versioning practices
- Review PRs and release notes for clarity, completeness, and accuracy
- Work with QA Engineers to document known issues and workarounds
- Champion knowledge management and discoverability across the team

### Goals
- Ensure every feature ships with accurate, accessible documentation
- Reduce support burden by providing self-service resources
- Build a living knowledge base that scales with the product

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning to identify documentation work items for each feature
- Review comments on PRs and design documents
- Doc review sessions with subject-matter experts before release

### Collaboration
- **Developers**: Gather technical details for API docs and runbooks; review code comments and PR descriptions to maintain doc accuracy.
- **Product Managers**: Align documentation scope to product goals; incorporate user feedback to improve content quality.
- **Project Managers**: Track documentation tasks on the project board; communicate any content dependencies that could delay a release.
